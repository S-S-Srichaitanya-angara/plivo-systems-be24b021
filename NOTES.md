# NOTES

Each frame is sent once with an 8-byte header (seq, type, group size); every group of 3 consecutive frames also gets one XOR-parity packet, so a single loss per group is reconstructed from the other frames plus parity. The receiver forwards every frame the instant it has it (received or reconstructed) with no jitter buffer, since the scorer only checks arrival-before-deadline, not order. Bandwidth overhead is a fixed 4/3 ≈ 1.33–1.40x, well under the 2.0x cap, leaving room for a harsher unseen profile. We recommend grading at **delay_ms=120**: it clears both profile A (0.13% misses) and profile B (0.73% misses) with real margin, versus e.g. 60ms on A which passes at only 0.93% — too close to the 1% cap to trust on an unseen profile. What breaks this design: two losses landing inside the same group of 3 are unrecoverable by XOR (probability rises quickly if loss rate exceeds ~B's 5%, or if losses arrive in correlated bursts rather than independently); a profile with a wider jitter tail than B's 80ms max delay would also need more than 120ms of playout delay to stay valid. Retransmission-based recovery was considered but rejected: a NACK-and-resend round trip costs two hostile-relay crossings, which risks exceeding our delay budget entirely.
